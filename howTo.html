The Grand Slam Guide to Tennis Basics

  • **The Grip:** Hold the racket using a comfortable grip, typically the Eastern or Semi-Western, for forehands and backhands. A good grip is key for control!
  • **The Serve (The Start):** Stand behind the baseline. Toss the ball high with your non-hitting hand, and strike it at its peak with an overhand motion, aiming for the service box diagonally opposite you.
  • **The Ready Position:** Stand in the center of the baseline with your knees slightly bent, feet shoulder-width apart, and the racket held in front of you. This is where you wait for the return.
  • **The Forehand:** When the ball comes to your dominant side, step forward, turn your shoulders, and swing the racket from low to high across your body, following through over the opposite shoulder.
  • **The Backhand:** When the ball comes to your non-dominant side, you can use a one-handed or two-handed grip. Turn your shoulders and use a similar swing path to the forehand, driving the ball forward.
  • **Footwork:** Move your feet constantly! Take small, quick steps (the "shuffle") to get behind the ball and position your body correctly before hitting.
  • **Scoring:** Learn the unique tennis scoring system: Love (0), 15, 30, 40, Game. Deuce (40-40) and Advantage are next-level scoring concepts to learn as you progress.
  • **Practice & Play:** The best way to learn is to practice hitting drills and play short sets against friends. Repetition builds muscle memory!
