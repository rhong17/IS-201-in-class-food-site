Official Tennis Program Quick Start Guide

Your Foundation for Success on the Court

I. The Essential Fundamentals

  • The Ready Position: Always face the net with your knees slightly bent, feet moving subtly, and your racket held firmly in front of you. This ensures immediate reaction time.
  • The Basic Grip (Continental): For beginner consistency, a Continental (or "hammer") grip is recommended for serves and volleys, providing versatility across strokes.
  • The Serve Procedure: Begin by standing behind the baseline. Execute a controlled ball toss directly above your head and extend fully to strike the ball cleanly into the diagonal service box.
  • Forehand Mechanics: Initiate the stroke with a shoulder turn, preparing the racket. Step into the ball and drive the racket face forward, accelerating through contact with a controlled follow-through.
  • Backhand Strategy: Use either a two-handed or one-handed technique. The key is proper weight transfer—stepping forward and rotating your torso to generate power and stability.
  • Scoring and Etiquette: Understand the traditional scoring sequence (Love, 15, 30, 40, Game). Always call the score clearly before serving and respect the boundaries and calls of your opponent.
